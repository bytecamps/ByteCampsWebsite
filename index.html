About Us

Hi! We are Rosalie and Catelijne. We are currently a rising senior and junior at Pinewood High School in Los Altos. One of our biggest hobbies is programming, and we love to pass on this passion to others. We have been teaching programming to kids ages 8-15 for the past two years, and it has been a great success! Both of our instructors have been coding for multiple years, and took AP Computer Science Principles in high school.
We think that programming should be fun and strive to teach it that way. That way the kids will continue programming later, and make it their new hobby.


To include everyone in our camps, no matter their location, all of our camps and private lessons are offered on Zoom.

Partners

App Creation w/ Apple's SwiftUI

Target audience: kids 9-16 years old

Do you want to learn how to make your very own iOS apps using Apple's SwiftUI and Xcode?

We will teach you SwiftUI and Swift so you can become your very own iOS App Developer. You will also learn about programming fundamentals like variables, loops, if statements, functions, lists, etc. Throughout the private lessons (or camp), multiple apps will be built to show off! Examples include a dice app or even an interactive color game (with leaderboard) during the advanced lessons!

Famous companies such as Facebook, Instagram, or Uber use Swift too! By taking our lessons, you will be able to start building the app of your dreams!

Requirements:

  • A macbook w/ Xcode installed (instructions follow later)

Pricing for a private lesson: $45 per two hours

Pricing for a camp: $199 per week

Advanced iOS App Development

Target audience: everyone who has completed the iOS App Development Camp or took private lessons

During these lessons, you will learn how to make even more advanced apps using SwiftUI, Swift, and Xcode.

We will dive deeper into Swift and make a fun color game with leaderboard. For the leaderboard, we will even use Firebase Database to allow for a continuous leaderboard among users! We will also learn how to make more complex screens and apps using SwiftUI.

Requirements:

  • A macbook w/ Xcode installed

Pricing for a private lesson: $60 per two hours ($75 dollars per two hours for two people)

Pricing for a camp: $250 per week

Games w/ Python

Target audience: kids 8-16 years old

Do you want to know how to program your own games with the programming language Python?

We will teach you what Python is and how to program with it! No programming experience is required.

You will learn about programming concepts like variables, loops, if statements, functions, lists, etc. Together we will make an array of engaging games that will be sure to draw attention!

Sample games include a space shooting game, a bomb clock game, a color game, and tic tac toe.

Requirements:

  • A computer

Pricing for a private lesson: $45 per two hours

Pricing for a camp: $199 per week

Digital Drawing Star w/ Procreate

Target audience: kids 8-14 years old

Do you want to know how to use the app Procreate to draw? During this camp you will learn the ins and outs of Procreate. You do not have to be a star at art to make aw-inspiring art with this app, all you need is these lessons!

You will learn how to navigate Procreate and learn to use different brushes, layers, symmetry, liquidfication (marbling), etc. It will be combined with fun projects!

Requirements:

  • An ipad
  • the app Procreate (around ten dollars)
  • Optional but highly recommended: Apple Pencil

Pricing for a private lesson: $45 per two hours

Pricing for a camp: $199 per week

Build your own Websites

Target audience: kids 8-16 years old

Have you always wanted to build your own website?

We will teach you how to make your own websites using HTML, CSS, and Javascript. No prior programming experience is required!

Games for Kids w/ Scratch

Target audience: kids 7-10 years old

Frequently Asked Questions

Do you offer private lessons?

Yes! We offer private lessons for iOS App Development, Games w/ Python, Web Development, and Procreate. Just scroll up to learn more about what we offer and to schedule tutoring!

Do I need a computer/iPad? If so, what kind?

What device you need depends on what you are interested in. For the Python or Web Development, any computer is fine (Windows, Apple, etc). For the iOS App Development camp, you will need a macbook (no windows computers). Lastly, for the Procreate camp, you need an iPad with the app Procreate installed.

How many students are in a camp?

We have a maximum of four or five students per instructor.

Is this only offered to students located in the Bay Area?

No, people from all around the world can take these camps or private lessons. Everything is done online via Zoom, so location does not play a role.
